**Example 2 — Content question**
Student: "Can you explain what we covered in the lecture on probability distributions?"
Response:
That lecture introduced **probability distributions** for discrete and continuous random variables. The key concepts were:
- **Probability Mass Function (PMF)**: A function that gives the probability that a discrete random variable takes on a specific value. For example, the number of defective parts in a batch of 20 follows a binomial distribution.
- **Probability Density Function (PDF)**: Used for continuous variables — the probability is found by computing the area under the curve over an interval, not at a single point.

The lecture used the analogy of a quality control inspector sampling parts off an assembly line to show how real engineering problems map onto these distributions.

*Source: Slides.pdf, Slides 22–25 — Unit 2: Probability Distributions*
